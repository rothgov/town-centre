Open Spaces

At the heart of the town centre, overlooked by Rotherham Minster, Minster Gardens with its Heart of Steel it’s the perfect place to sit with a cup of coffee and the morning paper. The gardens have space for open-air events, lawns and a meadow area. It also features the original foundation stones from Rotherham’s market hall as seating.

Nearby, you will find All Saints’ Square, with its water feature and surrounding shops. A little further afield, on the edge of the town centre, is Clifton Park, covering 54 acres and featuring a splash park, small woodland, museum and theme park.

Looking forward, more of Rotherham’s open spaces are being developed.

Phase one of the public realm improvement scheme – part of the Rotherham Town Centre Masterplan – will see a wide range of improvements to College Street, Bridgegate, Howard Street (Frederick St to Market Square) and Effingham Street (All Saint's Square to Market Square).

The scheme will be funded from the £17 million Town Centre Investment Fund (TCIF) set up to deliver the Masterplan and includes contributions from the Future High Streets Fund (FHSF) and Transforming Cities Fund. The proposals include:

  • New short stay parking spaces along College Street to improve access to the heart of the Town Centre
  • Removal of planter beds and seating on Bridgegate, to help tackle anti-social behaviour and open up views of the Minster
  • Upgraded lighting to improve safety
  • Improved stall layout on Effingham Street to create more space for traders and more capacity for other street markets, including continental food markets
  • Infrastructure work to provide new power supplies for market stalls and events
  • Replacement street furniture, such as litter bins, signage and bollards
  • New user-friendly seating, in line with Standing Up for Sitting Down campaign standards

The multi million pound regeneration of Forge Island is already moving ahead with flood defence and enabling works underway. This will help deliver the full scheme which includes a cinema, food and drink outlets, a hotel, car park and new leisure facilities.

Black Cat Building Consultancy and Greig and Stephenson Architects have been appointed to work with Rotherham Council and stakeholders to help prepare and develop plans for the markets, to support the Council’s Future High Street Fund bid.

"As we deliver the town centre masterplan, new housing developments and the leisure scheme at Forge Island are taking shape. We are also committed to improving the streets in the town centre and are now able to bring forward the first proposals to do so."

Cllr Denise Lelliott — Rotherham Council's Cabinet Member for Jobs and the Local Economy
